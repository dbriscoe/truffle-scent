Silky Truffle Fettuccine

A glossy, restaurant-style pasta built around Salsa al Tartufo, fettuccine and a final whisper of white truffle oil.

Ingredients

  • 200g fettuccine
  • 2–3 tbsp Salsa al Tartufo
  • 1 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • 2–4 tbsp reserved pasta water
  • Parmesan, black pepper and a few drops of white truffle oil

Method

  1. Cook the fettuccine in well-salted water until just tender. Reserve a cup of pasta water.
  2. Warm butter or olive oil gently in a pan. Add Salsa al Tartufo and loosen with a splash of pasta water.
  3. Toss in the pasta until the sauce turns glossy and clings to each ribbon.
  4. Plate immediately. Finish with Parmesan, black pepper and a few drops of white truffle oil at the table.

LEE’s tip: Keep the heat low after adding truffle salsa. The aroma is more elegant when it blooms gently rather than boiling away.

Black Truffle Risotto

A slow, luxurious rice dish finished with truffle salsa and black truffle oil for depth and fragrance.

Ingredients

  • 180g risotto rice
  • 700ml warm stock
  • 1 small onion or shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp Salsa al Tartufo 5%
  • Butter, Parmesan and black truffle oil

Method

  1. Soften onion in butter or olive oil until translucent. Add rice and stir for one minute.
  2. Add warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring until each addition is absorbed.
  3. When the rice is creamy but still has a little bite, fold in Salsa al Tartufo 5%.
  4. Turn off the heat and finish with butter, Parmesan and a light drizzle of black truffle oil.

LEE’s tip: Warm your bowls before serving. Risotto cools quickly, and warm bowls keep the texture soft and luxurious.

Truffle Toast with Chilli

A fast appetiser that feels generous: crisp bread, savoury truffle, a touch of chilli and a polished garnish.

Ingredients

  • Good bread, sliced
  • Salsa al Tartufo
  • Carpaccio al Tartufo
  • Peperoncino or crushed chilli
  • Olive oil, herbs and a little shaved cheese

Method

  1. Toast bread until crisp at the edges but still tender inside.
  2. Brush lightly with olive oil and spread with a small spoon of truffle salsa.
  3. Add carpaccio slices, a tiny pinch of peperoncino and herbs.
  4. Cut into small pieces and serve warm as the first bite of the evening.

LEE’s tip: Keep the chilli restrained. The warmth should lift the truffle, not hide it.

Cheese & Truffle Board

A host-friendly board with truffle accents, warm bread and simple textures that feel abundant without being complicated.

Ingredients

  • Aged cheese and one soft cheese
  • White truffle condiment
  • Carpaccio al Tartufo
  • Olives, warm bread and crackers
  • Optional: a light drizzle of truffle or olive oil

Method

  1. Place cheeses first, leaving space for small bowls and bread.
  2. Spoon white truffle condiment into a small dish and add carpaccio slices beside it.
  3. Add warm bread, crackers and olives, keeping the board generous but uncluttered.
  4. Finish with a light drizzle at the table and let guests build their own bites.

LEE’s tip: Serve at room temperature. Cheese and truffle both become more expressive when they are not fridge-cold.
