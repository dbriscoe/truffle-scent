Serves 2 · 20 minutes

Silky Truffle Fettuccine

A glossy, restaurant-style pasta using fettuccine, truffle salsa and a restrained final drizzle of white truffle oil.

Ingredients

  • 180–220g fettuccine
  • 2 generous teaspoons Salsa al Tartufo
  • 1 tablespoon butter or mild olive oil
  • 2–3 tablespoons reserved pasta water
  • Parmesan, black pepper and a few drops of white truffle oil

Method

  1. Cook the fettuccine in well-salted water until just tender. Reserve a mug of pasta water before draining.
  2. Warm butter or olive oil gently in a pan. Add the truffle salsa and loosen with a spoonful of pasta water.
  3. Toss the pasta through the sauce until glossy, adding a little more pasta water as needed.
  4. Plate immediately. Finish with Parmesan, black pepper and only a few drops of white truffle oil at the table.

LEE’s tip: Keep the heat gentle once the truffle salsa is added. The aroma should rise softly, not cook away.

Black Truffle Risotto

A creamy, slow-stirred dish with deep truffle character and a polished finishing oil aroma.

Ingredients

  • 180g risotto rice
  • 750ml warm stock, adjusted to taste
  • 1 small onion or shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons Salsa al Tartufo 5%
  • Butter, Parmesan and black truffle oil to finish

Method

  1. Soften onion gently in butter or olive oil until translucent.
  2. Add rice and stir for one minute until the grains look glossy.
  3. Add warm stock a ladle at a time, stirring until each ladle is mostly absorbed.
  4. When the rice is creamy and just tender, fold in the truffle salsa and Parmesan.
  5. Rest for two minutes, then serve with a few drops of black truffle oil.

LEE’s tip: Taste the stock before seasoning. A good stock base can already carry salt and savoury depth.

Truffle Toast with Chilli

A warm starter for guests: crisp bread, truffle depth, visible slices and a tiny lift of chilli.

Ingredients

  • 4 slices good bread or baguette
  • Salsa al Tartufo 90g
  • Carpaccio al Tartufo
  • A pinch of peperoncino
  • Olive oil and a little grated cheese, optional

Method

  1. Toast the bread until the edges are crisp but the centre is still pleasant to bite.
  2. Brush lightly with olive oil, then spread a thin layer of truffle salsa.
  3. Add truffle slices where they can be seen, then finish with a tiny pinch of peperoncino.
  4. Serve while warm, ideally with a small cheese board or drinks.

LEE’s tip: Treat the chilli as perfume, not fire. A tiny amount makes the truffle feel brighter.

Serves 4 · 15 minutes

Cheese & Truffle Board

A low-cook, high-impact board for hosting, gifting or a relaxed weekend table.

Ingredients

  • Two cheeses: one soft and one aged
  • White truffle condiment
  • Carpaccio al Tartufo
  • Warm bread, crackers or crostini
  • Olive oil, olives, nuts or honey as accents

Method

  1. Place cheeses first, leaving space for small jars or ramekins.
  2. Spoon the white truffle condiment into a small dish and add truffle slices as a visible garnish.
  3. Add warm bread and a small bowl of olive oil for dipping.
  4. Finish with olives, nuts or a small sweet accent to balance the savoury truffle notes.

LEE’s tip: Keep the truffle elements visible. They are part of the flavour and the theatre of the board.

LEE’s pantry notes

Small habits that make the food taste more elegant.

01

Finish, don’t fry

Truffle oils are best added after cooking. Heat can flatten the aroma, so drizzle at the table or just before serving.

02

Reserve pasta water

A spoonful of starchy pasta water helps truffle salsa, pesto and olive oil become glossy rather than heavy.

03

Build gift bundles

For an easy gift, pair pasta with truffle salsa and a finishing oil. Add a serving suggestion so the recipient knows exactly how to use it.

04

Keep jars clean

Use a clean spoon, refrigerate opened jars where needed, and wipe lids before returning them to the fridge.
